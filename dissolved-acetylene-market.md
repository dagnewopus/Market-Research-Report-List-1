<p><p>The dissolved acetylene market primarily consists of two production methods: calcium carbide production and hydrocarbon pyrolysis production. Calcium carbide production involves reacting calcium carbide with water to generate acetylene gas, which is then dissolved for storage and transport. In contrast, hydrocarbon pyrolysis production involves the thermal decomposition of hydrocarbons, yielding acetylene as a byproduct. These methods cater to various industrial applications, including welding, cutting, and chemical synthesis, influencing market dynamics based on production efficiency and demand trends.</p></p>
