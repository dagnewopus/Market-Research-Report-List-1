<p><p>The digital textile printing dyes market encompasses various ink types tailored for specific fabric applications. Acid inks are primarily used for protein fibers like wool and silk, providing vibrant colors. Disperse inks are suited for synthetic fibers such as polyester. Dye-based inks offer versatility for a range of fabrics but may lack durability. Reactive inks bond chemically with fibers, making them ideal for cotton. Sublimation inks are specialized for polyester, transferring images through heat to create durable prints.</p></p>
