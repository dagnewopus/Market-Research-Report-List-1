<p><p>The marine inverter market is categorized into two main types: outboards and inboards. Outboard inverters are typically designed for portable, detachable engines used on smaller vessels, providing flexibility and ease of maintenance. Inboard inverters are integrated into larger boats, delivering power to internal systems and enhancing overall performance. Each type caters to specific marine applications, with outboards focusing on maneuverability and portability, while inboards emphasize durability and efficient power management for larger watercraft.</p></p>
