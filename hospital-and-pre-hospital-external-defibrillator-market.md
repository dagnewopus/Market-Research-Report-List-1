<p><p>The hospital and pre-hospital external defibrillator market encompasses two main types: Manual External Defibrillators (MEDs) and Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs). MEDs require trained professionals to analyze heart rhythms and deliver shocks, providing more control in critical situations. In contrast, AEDs are user-friendly devices designed for laypersons, automatically assessing the patient's condition and administering shocks as needed. Both units are crucial for treating life-threatening cardiac emergencies, with AEDs commonly deployed in public spaces and MEDs primarily used in clinical settings.</p></p>
