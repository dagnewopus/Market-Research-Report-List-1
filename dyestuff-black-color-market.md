<p><p>The dyestuff market for black color consists of various categories, including basic dyes, acid dyes, and disperse dyes. Basic dyes are cationic and highly soluble, suitable for synthetic fibers like acrylics. Acid dyes are anionic, ideal for protein and nylon fibers, providing vibrant colors and good washfastness. Disperse dyes, non-ionic in nature, are designed for polyester and other synthetic materials, offering excellent lightfastness. These dye types cater to diverse industrial applications, influencing textile, leather, and plastic industries.</p></p>
