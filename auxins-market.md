<p><p>Auxins are plant hormones that regulate growth and development, significantly impacting agricultural practices. The market can be divided into two main types: natural auxins and synthetic auxins. Natural auxins are derived from plant sources, such as indole-3-acetic acid (IAA), promoting root formation and cell elongation. In contrast, synthetic auxins are chemically manufactured, providing greater stability and versatility in applications like herbicides and growth regulators. Both types play essential roles in enhancing crop yields and improving agricultural productivity.</p></p>
