<p><p>The hydrophilic intraocular lens (IOL) market is divided into two main types: non-pre-loaded IOLs and pre-loaded IOLs. Non-pre-loaded IOLs require manual handling and insertion by the surgeon, providing flexibility but demanding more skill and time. In contrast, pre-loaded IOLs come packaged in a device that simplifies implantation, reducing the risk of contamination and streamlining the surgical process. This distinction influences surgeon preference, procedural efficiency, and overall patient outcomes in cataract procedures.</p></p>
