<p><p>Plastic bonded hard ferrite magnets are manufactured using two main types: injection molding and compression molding. Injection molding involves melting plastic and mixing it with ferrite powder, followed by injecting the mixture into molds, allowing for intricate shapes and designs. In contrast, compression molding involves compacting the powdered mix in a mold under high pressure, followed by curing. Both methods enable the production of cost-effective, lightweight magnets suitable for various applications like automotive, electronics, and consumer goods.</p></p>
